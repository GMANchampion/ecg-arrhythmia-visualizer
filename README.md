# ecg-arrhythmia-visualizer

An interactive ECG learning tool that generates simplified, mathematically modeled rhythm strips in the browser.

## Purpose
I made this project for my own benefit because I was curious to learn more about ECG rhythms due to my recent volunteering in hospitals. I had always seen the fancy-looking devices in TV shows or actual hospitals and figured that there was no point staying oblivious forever... so I created this project to teach myself about it and also create a fun interactive game of it.

This project is also meant to help others learn about ECG rhythms, cardiac electrophysiology, and how different heart conditions change the shape and timing of a heartbeat signal, because you can switch between different rhythms, watching the waveform change, and trying the diagnostic challenge mode very easily to stay engaged and build intuition for how normal and abnormal heart rhythms appear on an ECG.

## Background Information

ECG: Stands for electrocardiogram. You may have also seen something called an EKG, but these are the exact same thing. The only reason that the C and K are different is based on what language you use to define the term when creating the abbreviation (i.e., cardio vs kardio). It is a quick, painless, and noninvasive medical test that records the electrical activity of your heart. It captures the electrical impulses that travel through your heart muscle with every beat. Doctors use these signals to check your heart rate, find causes for chest pain, and diagnose conditions like arrhythmias or heart attacks.

On the ECG screen, which is seen in this project when run, there are lines with spikes and bumps that all have their own meanings and are a part of the heartbeat.

The main parts of a heartbeat are:
 - P wave: the top chambers of the heart prepare to squeeze
 - QRS complex: the bottom chambers of the heart squeeze
 - T wave: the heart resets and gets ready for the next beat

For someone with a healthy heart and normal (sinus) rhythm, these beats are evenly spaced, and the shape of each beat looks mostly the same. Normal sinus rhythm means the heart is beating normally. The beats are steady, and each beat has the usual P wave, QRS complex, and T wave.

Tachycardia means the heart is beating too fast. The ECG shape may still look mostly normal, but the beats are closer together.

Bradycardia means the heart is beating too slowly. The beat shape may still look normal, but there is more space between beats.

Atrial fibrillation, or AFib, means the heart rhythm is irregular. The beats do not happen evenly, and the normal P waves are hard to see or are missing.

Premature ventricular contractions, or PVCs, are early extra beats. They interrupt the normal rhythm and usually look wider or stranger than regular beats.

Ventricular tachycardia, or V-Tach, is a fast and dangerous-looking rhythm. The normal heartbeat shape disappears and is replaced by wide, repeated waves.
